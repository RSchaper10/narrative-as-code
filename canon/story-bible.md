# Story Bible

## Working Title

Signal Harbor

## Logline

When a civic archivist inherits a dead surveyor's notebook, she and a salvage diver uncover a hidden harbor-siren network built to monitor and contain a dangerous class of maritime signal events.

## Genre and Tone

- Coastal mystery
- Light speculative thriller
- Procedural but atmospheric
- Human-scale stakes with institutional pressure

## Core Premise

Signal Harbor's public storm-warning system sits on top of an older network hidden inside tunnels under the bluff. That older system was not designed only for weather. It was built to detect, route, and suppress anomalous audio events that seem to travel through water, metal, and weather fronts together.

## Canon Defaults

- Mara Vale is the lead archivist and the primary point of institutional memory.
- Ivo Mercer is a salvage diver with practical knowledge of the harbor's physical infrastructure.
- The lower tide station is real and intentionally omitted from current public records.
- The hidden network is active in the present day.
- The network still has a contemporary operator or steward.
- The strange signal should feel measurable before it feels supernatural.
- The project should preserve uncertainty about whether the signal is a natural phenomenon, a human system, or both.

## Themes

- Memory versus maintenance
- Public infrastructure versus private control
- What institutions hide for stability
- The ethics of preserving dangerous knowledge

## Guardrails

- Keep the town grounded and recognizable.
- Let mystery emerge through records, machinery, and procedure.
- Avoid turning the signal into magic exposition.
- Keep characters making choices under incomplete information.
