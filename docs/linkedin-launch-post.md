# LinkedIn Launch Post

## Primary Version

I just open-sourced **Narrative-as-Code Starter**, a local-first repo for authors, editors, and narrative teams who want a cleaner way to manage long-form story projects.

The idea is simple: keep chapters, canon, metadata, research, review materials, and audio prep in separate but connected source files so humans stay in charge and AI/agent workflows stay grounded.

What’s in the starter:

- chapter-based manuscript structure
- schema-backed metadata validation
- reproducible manuscript builds
- continuity and drift reporting
- project-level review artifacts
- audiobook and editor-review scaffolding
- agent/operator playbooks for chapter-scoped workflows

This is not “AI writes your novel.”

It’s a way to make narrative projects more coherent, reviewable, and production-ready without trapping the manuscript in one giant document.

If you work in fiction, editorial workflows, narrative production, or creative tooling, I’d love to hear what feels useful, missing, or commercially interesting.
